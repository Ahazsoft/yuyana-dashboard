Italy

Overview

This 7-day itinerary will take you through the best of Rome, Vatican City, and Venice, with an exploration of iconic landmarks, a deep dive into ancient history, and a day dedicated to shopping. Highlights include the Colosseum, Vatican Museums, St. Peter’s Basilica, and a visit to the romantic canals of Venice.

  • 7 Days
  • Italy

Tour Plan

01

Day 1: Arrival in Rome

Arrive in Rome and check into your hotel. After settling in, enjoy a leisurely walk through Piazza Navona and visit the Trevi Fountain, one of the most iconic fountains in the world. Enjoy a traditional Italian dinner at a local trattoria

02

Day 2: Ancient Rome and Colosseum

Explore ancient Rome by visiting the Colosseum, the Roman Forum, and Palatine Hill. Learn about the gladiatorial battles and the grandeur of the Roman Empire. End your day with a visit to Capitoline Hill for panoramic views of the city

03

Day 3: Vatican Museums and St. Peter's Bascilla

Devote this day to Vatican City. Begin with the world-renowned Vatican Museums, where you can admire masterpieces, including the Sistine Chapel. Continue to St. Peter’s Basilica and, if you wish, climb to the dome for a spectacular view over the city.

04

Day 4: Shopping in Rome

Today is dedicated to shopping! Explore the Via del Corso for high-street shopping, or head to Via Condotti for designer boutiques like Gucci, Prada, and Louis Vuitton. Don’t miss the Spanish Steps and the surrounding shopping area.

05

Day 5: Travel to Venice

Take a train or flight to Venice. Upon arrival, check into your hotel and start your Venetian adventure with a stroll around Piazza San Marco and a visit to the Basilica di San Marco. In the evening, enjoy a gondola ride along the canals.

06

Day 6: Exploring Venice

Explore Doge’s Palace and cross the famous Rialto Bridge. Enjoy lunch at a café along the Grand Canal and spend the afternoon exploring the narrow streets and alleys of Cannaregio or San Polo.

07

Day 7: Leisure Day and Department

Spend the morning shopping for Venetian crafts, such as Murano glass or Burano lace, before enjoying your final views of the canals. Depending on your flight time, you may have time to visit a local café or shop for last-minute souvenirs before heading to the airport for your departure
